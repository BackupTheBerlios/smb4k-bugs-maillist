Summary: A handy feature

Details: Hi there. I am using Smb4k and really find it the most reliable smb browser for linux, allthough I know there is one thing to make it even more useful.
On the larger smb networks (like on my university campus), there are people who share resources and those who don't. When i look for some new things shared i have to check out every station and it takes quite long to do it. That's why people on my network started to use a smb browser called NetLook by KurbetSoft (it's for Windows). It has a nice feature of list of shares, that groups all the shares it has acces to in one neat list, showing only the names of the shares and allowing to browse through it, so shares with names like movies, music etc. can be selected directly from the list. It comes in handy when e.g. Since were just looking for something new it doesn't really matter that much who it belongs to rather than what it is.
Another cool feature in NetLook is searching through scanning a range of ip's, to address the problem of misconfigured or impropertly firewalled master browsers (which happen to plague my campus network ;/) that make a whole workgroup dissapear!

If you would like to check out the NetLook thingy go to:
http://kurbetsoft.nm.ru/ 
and find it in Софт  (soft ;)
but the site is russian, so in case you've lost, here are the links to binaries:
http://kurbetsoft.nm.ru/soft/net/netlook.zip
and the sources (if they'd be of any use since it's windows):
http://kurbetsoft.nm.ru/soft/net/netlook_src.zip

After instalation of netlook edit netlook_.ini in the program's folder to change the language and rename it to netlook.ini. To set the range of ip's to be scanned edit ipscan.txt.
